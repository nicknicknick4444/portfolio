I have been learning to program on and off for several years, but made a serious go of learning python in January 2021. Since then I have practised every day, working my way through six books (three on the topic of python, and three Django.)

To this end, I have created some Django projects that demonstrate what I have learnt.

Upon finishing the Django books I felt something was missing from the tutorials: business logic. Beyong list views, logins and blog apps, I wanted to make things from my own imagination.

Starting with DJango's own Polls app tutorial, I began experimenting, which soon led me onto a whole new road of learning. Before I knew it, I was out on my own, leaving the tutorials behind and following my imagination.

I picked up a lot of vital skills while working in the resulting projects, such a function views, the use of cookies for persistence, pagination and the consumption of web APIs.

The discovery of function views was a particular turning point, since they satisfied my desire to move beyond ready-made class views. And once I found I could send a word or a number to the template, then I thought about about how could send a calculated value, or the output of an API, or the returned values of several helper functions...

My curiosity and self-learning has led me to create some projects. Some are fun and some are light-hearted; some are serious and downright useful. All of them represent great leaps forward not only in my knowledge of Django, but of the python programming language in general.

Please have a look at my projets - see if you can sort the numbers game into the correct order; find the best and worst places in the UK for a picnic do some sums on a customised calculator; or even lecture a total stranger on Twitter for their sloppy spelling!

! I hope you enjoy them as much as I enjoyed making them. I have a strong desire to become a profesional Django developer, and these projects aim to demonstrate that passion.
